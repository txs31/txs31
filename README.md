I think seriously about what technology does to people, organisations, and systems, not just what it can do for them. That distinction matters more than most in this industry are willing to admit.

My work sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, edge computing, and cloud architecture. Not as buzzwords. As disciplines that carry real consequence when handled carelessly, and real leverage when handled well.

---

## What I Care About

**Artificial Intelligence** — the architecture of reasoning machines, the ethics of autonomous decision-making, and the discipline required to deploy AI where it genuinely serves people rather than merely impressing them.

**Cybersecurity** — governance frameworks, threat modelling, and the organisational behaviour that makes or breaks a security posture. Technology alone does not protect anything. Judgement does.

**Edge Computing** — moving intelligence closer to the point of need, reducing latency, reducing exposure, and rethinking what it means to process data at the boundary of the network.

**Cloud & Infrastructure** — the foundations that everything else runs on. Designed well, they are invisible. Designed poorly, they are the thing you are explaining to the board at 2am.
